Cable

It uses a coaxial cable in which radio frequencies are transmitted. You can have signal transmission in both directions at the same time.

In the USA (main user) is used for television broadcasts and for its gaming system. Two-way Cable operators now offer customers advanced telecommunications services, including high-speed Internet access, digital cable television, and residential telephone service.

The reason for which you may offer different services is that each of them is on a different electromagnetic frequency.

Its components are:

  • Cable Modem Termination System (CMTS)
  • Cable Modem (CM) on the subscriber end

DOCSIS

DOCSIS provides great variety in options available at OSI layers 1 and 2, the physical and data link layers.

Physical layer

Channel width:

  • Downstream:6 MHz channels (e.g. North America) or 8 MHz channels ("EuroDOCSIS").
  • Upstream: DOCSIS 1.0/1.1 specifies channel widths between 200 kHz and 3.2 MHz. DOCSIS 2.0 also specifies 6.4 MHz.

DOCSIS

Data link layer

DOCSIS use a mix of deterministic access methods for upstream transmissions, with a limited use of contention for bandwidth requests. Due to this, it experiences relatively few collisions, in contrast to the pure contention-based MAC CSMA/CD employed in older Ethernet systems (there is no contention in switched Ethernet).

For DOCSIS 1.1 and above the MAC layer also includes extensive quality-of-service (QoS) features that help to efficiently support applications that have specific traffic requirements such as low latency, e.g. voice over IP.

DOCSIS 3.0 features channel bonding, which enables multiple downstream and upstream channels to be used together at the same time by a single subscriber.

DOCSIS

Throughput

30.72 Mbit/s per 6.4 MHz channel, or 10.24 Mbit/s per 3.2 MHz channel. All three versions of the DOCSIS standard support a downstream throughput with 256-QAM of up to 42.88 Mbit/s per 6 MHz channel, or 55.62 Mbit/s per 8 MHz channel for EuroDOCSIS.

Network layer

DOCSIS modems are managed via an IP address. DOCSIS 3.0 adds management over IPv6.

DSL

DLS provide high speed connections using copper wires used for telephony.

This is possible because voice use only a little portion of RF signal carried by copper wires (voice 300Hz~3kHz) and we can use the other frequency (3kHz-1MHz) for data.

It’s a connection that the ISP use in a loop, or last mile.

ADSL and SDLS can perform a maximum of 40Mb/s of bandwidth in a loop.

The component of a DLS connections are:

  • DLS Modem or TransRecever (users side)
  • DSLAM (Digital Subscriber Line Access Multiplexer) to combine multiple DLS in a larger connection (ISP side)

Separating Voice and Data in ADSL

The DSL use a single pair of wire for costumers, in this wire can be shared voice and data.

To separe data from voice, that can be alterated by data frequency, can be used:

  • A Microfilter
    • Low passed filter that allow to use any telephone jack to connect DLS Modem
  • A Splitter
    • Separe Date and voice in 2 line

Comparing Broadband Solutions

Factors that can be used for media choice:

Cable Bandwidth is shared by many users, upstream data rates are often slow
DSL
Fiber-to-the-Home Limited bandwidth that is distance sensitive, upstream rate is proportionally quite small compared to downstream rate
Cellular/Mobile Requires fiber installation directly to the home
Wi-Fi Mesh Coverage is often an issue, even within a SOHO, bandwidth is relatively limited
WiMAX Bit rate is limited to 2 Mb/s per subscriber, cell size is 1 to 2 km.
Satellite Expensive, limited capacity per subscriber; often provides access where no other access is possible.
